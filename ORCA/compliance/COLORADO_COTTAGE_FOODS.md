## 🏛️ Executive Summary (Colorado Cottage Food Act)

To operate a baking/cooking startup from a home kitchen in Colorado, you must comply with the **Colorado Cottage Foods Act**. This allows you to sell certain "non-potentially hazardous" foods directly to consumers without a commercial kitchen license, provided you complete a food safety course.

## 🚀 Tactical Roadmap (Boulder, CO)

### Phase 1: Immediate Certification

- [ ] **Enroll in CSU Extension Cottage Food Safety Training.**
  - *Link:* [extension.colostate.edu](https://extension.colostate.edu/offering/cottage-food-safety-training/)
  - *Action:* Check for the next available live Zoom session.
- [ ] **Interim Check:** If a CSU class isn't available for 2+ weeks and you need to start TODAY, complete the [StateFoodSafety](https://www.statefoodsafety.com/food-handler/colorado/state-of-colorado) course (45 mins).

### Phase 2: Operations & Labeling (CO Requirements)

Once certified, every product sold under the "ORCA/Vayne" baking banner MUST include:

1. **Identity:** Name of the product.
2. **Producer Info:** Your name, address (or permit #), and contact info.
3. **Ingredients:** Full list in descending order by weight.
4. **Allergens:** Clear declaration of major allergens (Milk, Eggs, Nuts, Wheat, etc.).
5. **MANDATORY DISCLAIMER:**  
   *"This product was produced in a home kitchen that is not subject to state licensure or inspection. This product is not intended for resale."*

### Phase 3: Boulder County Specifics

- [ ] **Sales Tax:** Register for a Colorado Sales Tax License.
- [ ] **Zoning:** Verify Boulder municipal codes for "Home Occupations." Generally, home-based baking is permitted if it doesn't create significant traffic or noise.
