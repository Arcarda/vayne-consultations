Project Title: Optimizing Shelter Occupancy via Real-Time Data

Date: February 7, 2026

Technology Partner: Vayne Consulting

1. The Problem: "Hidden Vacancy"

Montreal’s shelter network operates with critical opacity. While beds are nominally "full," administrative lags and lack of real-time synchronization mean that beds often sit empty for hours or overnight while individuals sleeping rough are turned away.

Without a centralized, real-time view of network capacity, we are effectively choosing to leave people on the street when resources exist.

2. The Innovation: Synchro-Refuge

Synchro-Refuge is not a new Case Management System. It is a lightweight Availability Layer that sits on top of existing systems.

  • Aggregator API: Connects to disparate shelter management inputs to pull a single metric: Current Bed Availability.
  • Command Dashboard: Provides mobile intervention teams with a live, map-based view of exactly where beds are open.
  • Instant Distribution: Eliminates the "call-around" delay. When a bed opens, mobile teams know instantly.

3. HRIF Alignment

This project is precision-engineered for the Homelessness Reduction Innovation Fund (HRIF) Round 3 priorities:

  • Priority Population: Directly targets Unsheltered Homelessness by reducing the friction between "Street" and "Bed."
  • Outcome Measure: The primary metric is Diversion — successfully moving individuals from the street into existing network capacity.
